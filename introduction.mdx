Allsky is an open-source software system that transforms a Raspberry Pi and a compatible camera into a fully automated all-sky observatory. It captures wide-angle sky images throughout the night, processes them into keograms, startrails, and timelapse videos, and can publish them to a local or remote website — all automatically.

## What Allsky does

Allsky runs continuously on your Raspberry Pi, handling every aspect of sky imaging automatically:

- **Captures images** using ZWO ASI cameras or Raspberry Pi camera modules throughout the night
- **Processes each image** through a customizable module pipeline — adding overlays, counting stars, detecting meteors, and more
- **Generates nightly products** including keogram timelines, startrail composites, and timelapse videos
- **Publishes to websites** via FTP or SCP to a local Pi website or a remote server
- **Provides a WebUI** for live monitoring, image browsing, and full configuration

## Get up and running

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check requirements">
    Confirm you have a supported Raspberry Pi (Zero 2, 2, 3, 4, or 5) running Raspberry Pi OS, and a compatible ZWO or Raspberry Pi camera. See [Requirements](/getting-started/requirements).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install Allsky">
    Run the installer script on your Raspberry Pi. The process takes 15–30 minutes and sets up the software, WebUI, and all dependencies. See [Installation](/getting-started/installation).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your camera">
    Open the WebUI at `http://your-pi-ip/` and configure your camera type, exposure settings, and upload destinations. See [Allsky Settings](/configuration/allsky-settings).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start capturing">
    Allsky starts automatically after installation. Point your camera at the sky and images will begin appearing in the WebUI within seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The WebUI includes a direct link to this documentation. You can also find help, ask questions, and report issues on the [Allsky GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/AllskyTeam/allsky/discussions) page.
</Note>
